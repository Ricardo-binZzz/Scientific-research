# Manuscript Authoring

## Goal

Keep Word writing structured, traceable, and easy to revise by chapter.

## Writing Rules

- Draft one chapter or subsection at a time.
- Keep headings, numbering, and figure labels consistent across the manuscript.
- Use Zotero as the citation source of truth.
- Do not mix raw notes and final prose in the same paragraph.

## Word Style Rules

- Use a fixed heading hierarchy.
- Keep figure captions descriptive and self-contained.
- Keep table captions short but specific.
- Number equations, figures, and tables consistently.

## Revision Rule

- Each edit session should declare the exact scope before changes start.
- Anything outside that scope stays untouched unless it blocks correctness.

## Citation Check

- Every in-text citation must appear in the bibliography.
- Check for duplicated keys, missing fields, and inconsistent citation format before export.
